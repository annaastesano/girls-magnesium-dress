Four Dimensions of digital maturity

We measure data maturity across four different dimensions. Based on your strategic choices, not all of the dimensions may be applicable to your organisation.

Why digital maturity matters

Consumer shift

Today, more and more of our lives are powered by mobile and digital. We love our devices, but what we really love is the power our devices give us. The power to get exactly what we want, instantly and effortlessly. To have our needs not just met, but anticipated.

For marketers, this means the very nature of how consumers behave and what they expect has changed. And as these expectations ratchet up, so do the requirements (and opportunities) for marketers.

Today’s consumers are:

  • Curious

    They turn to Google Search more and more often looking for to get useful advice and answers - even for the small stuff - so every decision they make is an informed one.

    • In the last 2 years, mobile searches have risen 239% for best travel accessories, 208% for best face powder and 156% for best wet weather gear. 1
    • Mobile searches for “baby shopping lists” and “healthy shopping lists” are up 180% in the last 2 years. 2

  • Demanding

    They want relevant and tailor-made experiences and expect brands to understand and meet their intent with useful information in the moment.

    • "Near me" searches have grown 4.5x on Google search and 6x on Google Maps on mobile in the last 2 years in EMEA. 3
    • 89% of marketers say anticipating customer needs and providing helpful experiences along the consumer journey is critical to their brand’s growth. 4

  • Impatient

    People are making decisions faster than ever before, and they expect to be able to act on those decisions instantly.

    • 1 in 2 visits are abandoned if a mobile site takes longer than 3 seconds to load. 5

So, how can you be digitally mature and so anticipate the needs of your customers today?

Be there

Use the power of technology and insights to deliver relevant, real-time messages at scale, whenever each consumer is ready for them.

Be helpful

Build a strategy driven by assistance, reorienting to become more relevant across the customer experience.

Be quick

Deliver the speedy, frictionless experiences on mobile that consumers expect.

Of course, if customers benefit, businesses benefit - but they’re struggling to put it all into practice. Our research with BCG shows only 2% of brands are realising the full potential of data-driven marketing.
